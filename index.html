Sylvester Stallone

The Italian Stallion who revolutionized action cinema

Here's a timeline of Sly's remarkable life:

  • 1946 - Born in Hell's Kitchen, New York City
  • 1969 - Makes his film debut in The Party at Kitty and Stud's, later working as a theater usher while pursuing his acting dreams
  • 1974 - Writes the script for Rocky after watching the Muhammad Ali vs. Chuck Wepner fight, reportedly completing it in just three days
  • 1976 - Despite severe financial hardship, refuses to sell the Rocky script unless he can star in it. The film is made for $1 million and goes on to earn $225 million worldwide
  • 1977 - Rocky wins three Academy Awards, including Best Picture, and receives ten nominations, including Best Actor and Best Original Screenplay for Stallone
  • 1982 - Debuts as John Rambo in First Blood, creating another iconic character and franchise
  • 1985 - Rocky IV becomes one of the highest-grossing films of the year and a cultural phenomenon
  • 1993 - Receives critical acclaim for his performance in Cliffhanger and Demolition Man
  • 2006 - Makes an emotional return to his signature role in Rocky Balboa, proving his enduring appeal
  • 2015 - Returns to the Rocky franchise in Creed, earning a Golden Globe nomination and critical praise
  • 2019 - Concludes the Rambo saga with Last Blood, marking over four decades as the character
  • 2023 - Continues to inspire audiences through his work in both film and television

"I am not the richest, smartest or most talented person in the world, but I succeed because I keep going and going and going."

-- Sylvester Stallone
